System Design III: Page 7 - Reliability & Recovery

Designing for Failure

In distributed systems, failure is inevitable. Servers crash, networks partition, and disks fail. A reliable system is not one that never fails, but one that handles failure gracefully. Reliability is achieved through redundancy (having extra components ready), failover (automatically switching to those extra components), and a well-defined Disaster Recovery (DR) plan to restore data and services after catastrophic events.

1. Key Reliability Metrics

  • RTO (Recovery Time Objective): The maximum acceptable downtime after a failure. How fast must the system be back up?
  • RPO (Recovery Point Objective): The maximum acceptable data loss (in time). How much data can you afford to lose?

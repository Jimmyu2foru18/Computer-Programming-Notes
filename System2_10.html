System Design II: Page 10 - Backend: Microservices

Scaling Complexity

A Microservices Architecture decomposes a large application into a collection of small, autonomous services, each responsible for a specific business capability (e.g., Auth, Catalog, Cart). This allows teams to develop, deploy, and scale these services independently. However, this architectural freedom comes at the price of massive operational complexity in networking, data consistency, and distributed tracing.

1. Key Pillars of Microservices

  • Independence: Each service can use its own language, framework, and database.
  • Communication: Sync (REST/gRPC) for immediate responses; Async (Queues) for eventual consistency.
  • Orchestration: Tools (Kubernetes) manage the lifecycle (deployment/scaling) of dozens or hundreds of services.

System Design III: Page 1 - Design: Scalability

Handling Growth

A system that works for 100 users might crash with 100,000. Scalability is the ability of a system to handle increased load (more users, more data, more requests) by adding resources. A scalable system doesn't just get faster; it remains performant and reliable even as demand grows. Design decisions regarding scalability are foundational and must be made early.

1. The Two Ways to Scale

  • Vertical Scaling (Scale Up): Adding more power (CPU, RAM) to a single machine. Simple, but eventually hits a physical hardware limit and creates a single point of failure.
  • Horizontal Scaling (Scale Out): Adding more machines to the pool. Much harder to design for (requires shared state/statelessness), but offers virtually unlimited growth.

System Design III: Page 6 - Observability

The Three Pillars

In a distributed system, you cannot debug by logging into a single server and running tail -f /var/log/syslog. You need Observability—the capability to understand the internal state of your system just by looking at its outputs. Observability is often broken down into three pillars: Logs (discrete events), Metrics (aggregated numerical data over time), and Tracing (following a request as it moves through multiple microservices).

1. The Pillars

  • Logs: Records of specific events (e.g., "User 123 logged in").
  • Metrics: Numerical data points aggregated over time (e.g., "Average CPU usage over 5 minutes").
  • Tracing: Tracking a request's journey across services (e.g., Service A → Service B → Database).

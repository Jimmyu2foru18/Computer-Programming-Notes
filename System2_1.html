System Design II: Page 1 - Backend: Server Paradigms

The Foundation of Server-Side Logic

The choice of server-side paradigm dictates how your application scales, how it's deployed, and how services communicate. As a system designer, you must choose between the simplicity of a Monolith, the flexibility of Microservices, or the cost-efficiency of Serverless based on your specific traffic patterns and team size.

1. Architecture Patterns

  • Monolithic: All logic in one codebase. Fast to start, easy to test, but becomes a "big ball of mud" as you add features.
  • Microservices: Decomposed into small, independent services. Enables independent scaling and technology choices, but introduces massive overhead in networking and data consistency.
  • Serverless (FaaS): You write functions; the cloud provider manages the infrastructure. Perfect for event-driven tasks, but watch out for cold-start latency.

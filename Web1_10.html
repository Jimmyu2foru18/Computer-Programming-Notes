Your Digital Calling Card

A portfolio is the most critical asset for a web developer. It’s not just a collection of links; it’s proof of your ability to design, structure, style, and make interactive websites. In this capstone project, you will build a personal portfolio from scratch using the HTML, CSS, and JavaScript skills you've mastered. This project will serve as the foundation for your professional presence online.

1. Project Blueprint & Requirements

Your portfolio should demonstrate your proficiency in key areas:

  • Responsive Layout: Must look professional on mobile, tablet, and desktop.
  • Project Showcase: A gallery of at least 3-4 projects with descriptions, tech stacks, and links.
  • Contact Form: A functional form that collects user input.
  • Interactive Elements: At least one dynamic feature (e.g., dark/light theme toggle, smooth scrolling navigation).

Real-World Relationship: The Artist's Gallery

Think of your Portfolio like an Artist's Gallery Show. If you are a painter, you wouldn't just tell people you can paint; you would display your best work in frames on a wall in a clean, well-lit gallery. Your portfolio is your gallery. The HTML is the wall, the CSS is the lighting and frame, and the JavaScript is the spotlight that makes the work pop when the viewer approaches it. A messy, poorly organized gallery will make people doubt your skills, while a clean, professional gallery showcases your talent at its absolute best.

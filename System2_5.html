System Design II: Page 5 - DB: NoSQL Modeling

Flexibility at Scale

NoSQL databases (like MongoDB, Cassandra, DynamoDB) offer an alternative to the rigid structure of SQL. They are designed for flexibility, horizontal scalability, and high performance. By storing data as documents, key-value pairs, or graphs, NoSQL databases allow your schema to evolve rapidly as your application changes, making them the preferred choice for massive, distributed, and highly dynamic datasets.

1. Why NoSQL?

  • Schema-less: Documents can have different structures. You aren't stuck with fixed columns.
  • Horizontal Scaling: Easily scale across hundreds of servers (sharding) without the complexity of SQL partitioning.
  • Performance: Optimized for specific access patterns (e.g., massive key-value lookups).

// Example: MongoDB Document
{
  "_id": 1,
  "name": "User Name",
  "preferences": { "theme": "dark" } // Embedded data
